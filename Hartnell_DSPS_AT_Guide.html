Screen Readers, Lecture Capture, and AI Reading Tools — Counselor Reference Guide

Tool Profiles Quick Reference Matrix Decision Guide

This guide helps DSPS counselors and disability staff match students with the most appropriate assistive technology based on their disability, tech comfort level, and primary use case.

The five tools covered represent the core AT options for reading support and lecture access. Tools are not mutually exclusive — many students benefit from using two in combination (e.g., Genio + Otter AI for students who need both lecture recording and live captions).

Tool Profiles

📖
Kurzweil 3000
Full-page visual reading with copyright protection
Platform
Windows / Mac (limited mobile)
Tech Level
Moderate — requires onboarding
Content Type
PDFs, textbooks, scanned pages
AI Features
No — traditional TTS engine
Lecture Capture
No
Overview

Kurzweil 3000 is the gold standard for text-to-speech reading in academic settings. It displays the actual source document — textbook pages, scanned PDFs, course packets — while simultaneously highlighting each word as it is read aloud. Files are stored in a proprietary KES format, giving institutions meaningful copyright protection over alternate media they produce and distribute.

Strengths
  • Displays actual page layout while reading
  • Word-by-word highlighting supports decoding
  • Proprietary KES format protects copyright
  • Study tools: notes, highlights, vocabulary
  • Strong DSPS institutional track record
Limitations
  • Desktop-first — not a mobile solution
  • Steeper learning curve; needs onboarding
  • No AI summarization or Q&A
  • Requires alternate media prepared in advance
Best fit — student profile

Students with print disabilities who benefit from seeing the actual page while listening. Ideal for extended academic reading — textbooks, long articles — where visual anchoring to page layout matters. Also the strongest choice when copyright-controlled alternate media distribution is a concern.

Not the right fit when...

Students need mobile-first tools, real-time AI interaction, or lecture recording. Not appropriate as a quick on-ramp for low-tech students.

Speechify
Fast, mobile-first listening — snap a photo and go
Platform
iOS, Android, Chrome extension
Tech Level
Low — very easy to start
Content Type
Photos, PDFs, web pages, ebooks
AI Features
Limited — speed controls, AI voices
Lecture Capture
No
Overview

Speechify is the most accessible on-ramp for students who need audio support quickly and without complexity. Its standout feature is the ability to photograph a printed page with a smartphone and hear it read aloud immediately via OCR. It supports a wide range of formats but is designed for listening, not deep study workflows.

Strengths
  • Photograph printed handouts for instant audio
  • Extremely simple mobile UI — minimal learning curve
  • Wide format support: web, PDF, docs, ebooks
  • High-quality natural-sounding AI voices
  • Free tier available; broad device compatibility
Limitations
  • No visual display of source material
  • Premium features require subscription
  • No note-taking, study tools, or lecture recording
  • Less suited to extended academic workflows
Best fit — student profile

Students who are tech-averse and need a fast on-ramp with minimal setup. Perfect for students who receive printed handouts in class and want to photograph them for immediate audio access. Strong first tool for students new to AT.

DyslexiaADHDLow Tech ConfidenceAny print-access need
Not the right fit when...

Students need deep study workflows, visual page anchoring, lecture capture, or a tool with institutional copyright controls.

🧠
Tailo
AI-powered reading with summaries and comprehension support
Platform
Web, mobile app
Tech Level
Low-moderate — intuitive UI
Content Type
Uploaded texts, PDFs, articles
AI Features
Strong — summaries, simplification, Q&A
Lecture Capture
No
Overview

Tailo is an AI-powered academic reading platform built specifically for disabled and neurodivergent students. Rather than displaying the original source page, it processes uploaded text and provides AI-generated summaries, simplified language versions, and interactive Q&A. Students can ask questions about a reading directly. Winner of the Scope Accessible Product Award 2025.

Strengths
  • AI summarizes dense academic texts
  • Automatically simplifies complex language
  • Interactive Q&A about reading content
  • Built specifically for neurodivergent students
  • Award-winning accessibility design (Scope 2025)
Limitations
  • Students not seeing the original page layout
  • Not for page-referenced citations or visual layout tasks
  • AI may miss nuance in technical/scientific texts
  • Newer platform — less US community college track record
Best fit — student profile

Students with cognitive disabilities, ADHD, or learning disabilities who get overwhelmed by dense academic language. The AI layer helps surface key ideas quickly. Valuable for neurodivergent students comfortable with AI tools who prefer asking follow-up questions over re-reading.

ADHDCognitive DisabilityLearning DisabilityNeurodivergentProcessing Speed
Not the right fit when...

Students need to read pages with precise visual or layout context, work with page numbers for citations, or require in-class lecture support.

🎙️
Genio (formerly Glean)
Lecture recording with AI notes, outlines, and study quizzes
Platform
Web, iOS, Android
Tech Level
Moderate — structured workflow
Content Type
Live lectures, audio, slide decks
AI Features
Moderate — post-lecture transcript, outlines, quizzes
Lecture Capture
Yes — audio + synced notes + post-lecture AI transcript
Overview

Genio is the leading institutionally-deployed lecture capture and study tool in DSPS environments. Students record lectures while adding timestamped notes; after class, Genio generates a full transcript, AI-structured outline, and study quiz. Slide decks can be imported and synced to recordings. Important: Genio does NOT produce real-time captions — transcription happens post-lecture only. Recordings cannot be downloaded or shared outside the app, addressing faculty privacy concerns.

Strengths
  • Records audio synced with student notes
  • Post-lecture AI transcript, outline, and quiz
  • Import and sync slide decks to recordings
  • Recordings cannot be downloaded or shared
  • Widely adopted in UC/CSU DSPS programs
  • Works in-person, online, and hybrid
Limitations
  • Transcription is NOT real-time — post-lecture only
  • NOT a live captioning tool for DHH students
  • Requires faculty permission protocol
  • License-based — must be provisioned by institution
Best fit — student profile

Students whose disability affects real-time note-taking: ADHD, processing speed differences, learning disabilities, anxiety, or physical disabilities limiting writing. Best when the student is engaged in lecture but cannot simultaneously write. The post-class AI outline and quiz make it a full study support system.

ADHDProcessing SpeedLearning DisabilityAnxietyPhysical Disability
Not the right fit when...

Deaf or Hard of Hearing students who need live captions during class — use Otter AI instead. Genio can complement Otter for post-lecture review.

📝
Otter AI
Real-time transcription for lectures and online classes
Platform
Web, iOS, Android
Tech Level
Low — minimal setup
Content Type
Live speech, Zoom/Meet, recorded audio
AI Features
Strong — live captions, summary, speaker ID
Lecture Capture
Yes — real-time live transcription + post-session summary
Overview

Otter AI provides real-time speech-to-text transcription, making it the primary tool for Deaf and Hard of Hearing students who need live caption access during class. It integrates directly with Zoom and Google Meet for online and hybrid courses. Unlike Genio, Otter produces captions in real time. It also generates post-session summaries and identifies multiple speakers — useful in discussion-heavy classes.

Strengths
  • Real-time transcription — live captions during class
  • Integrates with Zoom and Google Meet
  • Speaker identification for multi-voice discussions
  • Post-session AI summary and keyword highlights
  • Free tier available; student-friendly pricing
Limitations
  • No study quiz, note-syncing, or slide import
  • Can struggle with accents or technical vocabulary
  • Cloud-stored transcripts — review privacy policy
  • Less structured for deep academic study workflows
Best fit — student profile

Deaf or Hard of Hearing students who need live captions during in-person or online class — this is Otter's primary DSPS use case. Also valuable for online courses needing Zoom caption access, or any student who wants searchable text notes without the full Genio workflow.

Deaf / Hard of HearingAuditory ProcessingLive Captions
Not the right fit when...

Students need structured study tools, slide syncing, or comprehensive note-taking workflows. Use Genio instead — or both tools together when live and post-lecture access are both needed.
